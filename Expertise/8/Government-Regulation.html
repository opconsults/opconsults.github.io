As a respected and established UAE law firm, we perform a “trusted advisor” role to federal and local governmental authorities on a range of matters.

We are regularly appointed to establish governmental and quasi-governmental institutions and corporations, to review and prepare laws and regulations to support a range of governmental initiatives, including financial regulation and structuring, formation and conduct of special economic zones, environmental preservation, privatisation projects, and the development of policies and procedures.

Our deep understanding and considerable contacts in the governmental arena undoubtedly enhance our ability to provide meaningful, considered advice to our commercial clients in all our practice areas.
 

Our services include:

  • legislative drafting
  • incorporation including drafting of policies and manuals
  • employment regulations
  • drafting of tender documentation and contracts
  • governance and probity
  • asset sales, privatisation and nationalisation
  • infrastructure development
  • project finance.

  • NEW YORK CONVENTION: LACK OF JURISDICTION PREVENTS ENFORCEMENT IN UAE

    13 Oct 2013

    The arbitration enforcement proceedings brought by Construction Company International (CCI) against the Ministry of Irrigation of the Government of Sudan (MOI), for whom our firm acted, has now passed through all three tiers of the Dubai Courts. The Dubai Court of Cassation (Case No. 156/2013 Civil Cassation) rejected CCI’s arguments and upheld the judgments of the lower Courts. This means that all three tiers of the Dubai Courts, having considered the applicability of the New York Convention, refused to recognise and enforce two ICC Paris arbitration awards on the ground that under the UAE’s procedural laws the Court had no jurisdiction. This result is likely to be viewed with concern by arbitration practitioners based in Dubai and worldwide.
