Our lawyers have considerable experience and expertise in structuring and negotiating large and complex projects across a range of industries, utilities and infrastructure, including the oil and gas, water, power, industrial and petrochemical sectors.

Ommar Platinum Consults has a pre-eminent track record in acting for project sponsors and financiers in the UAE power and water industry, as well as advising in other groundbreaking public private partnerships (PPPs) and project funding structures in a range of sectors.

The UAE is a leading international energy producer and investor, and our team advises on projects and investments in the upstream, midstream and downstream sectors (including projects and investments with a cross-border element).

In the upstream sector, we have experience of various forms of concession, licensing and production sharing agreements, joint operating and joint venture agreements, farm-ins, farm-outs and other forms of investment participation and unitisation arrangements. In the midstream and downstream sectors, we have advised on transportation and processing contracts, product sale and purchase agreements, marketing, distribution and agency arrangements, and energy storage transactions.

We have represented clients in some of the region’s largest projects including the GCC’s first cross border processed gas transmission project and the largest energy-related venture ever undertaken in the region. Our team has been involved in major transactions in various international jurisdictions including in the UAE, Iran, Iraq, Yemen, Egypt, Oman, Qatar, Libya, Sudan, Malaysia, Pakistan, India, Vietnam, the United Kingdom, Norway, Papua New Guinea and the Falkland Islands.

Many of our lawyers have extensive experience working as in-house counsel to international oil and gas companies. As such, we offer a comprehensive start to finish service ensuring our clients’ business objectives are met whilst taking into consideration the requirements of other stakeholders throughout the life of the project.

We advise on:

  • formation of consortia into joint venture legal entities to acquire energy assets or to build, own, operate, finance, and develop energy projects
  • project finance and refinancing
  • evaluation, acquisition and disposal of oil and gas assets
  • various forms of production sharing agreements; operating and joint venture agreements; farm-ins; unitisations; transportation and processing agreements; crude oil and product sale and purchase agreements; joint marketing, distribution, agency, and fuel depot agreements; crude oil and product sales agreements; technical and commercial services agreements; project management services agreements and operation and maintenance services agreements; tariff agreements, tolling agreements, power purchase agreements, fuel supply agreements, and gas sale and purchase agreements/offtake agreements
  • various types of EPIC and EPC contracts for major construction activities for petroleum ventures
  • competitive tendering and probity
  • environmental compliance and surveying agreements
  • procurement of environmental licenses and approvals and the preparation and negotiation of requirements for Environmental Impact Assessments
  • the pursuit and defence of contractual claims before courts and tribunals

  • CURRENCY DE-PEGGING: POSSIBLE CONTRACTUAL PROVISIONS IN THE ENERGY SECTOR

    11 Apr 2016

    In brief The dramatic fall in crude prices has led to speculation that some oil-producing countries may be forced to abandon their currencies’ fixed exchange rate pegs, which are typically set to the US Dollar. This arises as the fall in oil revenues results in a ballooning budget deficit in a country which is heavily dependent on oil production, causing that country to deplete its foreign reserves. This, in turn, leads to pressure on the oil-producing country to devalue its own currency.
