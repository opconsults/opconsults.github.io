JAMIE LIDDINGTON

Partner, Head of Employment

Dubai

+971 4 4292999

j.liddington@opconsults.com
  download vcard

Jamie is Head of the Employment department in Dubai. He has considerable experience in advising upon the full range of contentious and non-contentious employment matters. He regularly advises major multinational companies in regard to their day-to-day employment queries as well as providing key support in relation to the employment aspects of mergers and acquisitions and other types of corporate transactions.

Jamie has particular expertise in employment litigation and he has successfully represented clients in the High Court and Employment Tribunals (England & Wales) as well as the Dubai Labour Courts and the DIFC Courts (where he has rights of audience).

Jamie regularly presents at industry events in the UAE and is also a contributor to the Employment & Labour Law – International Series (sixth edition) by Sweet & Maxwell as well as the PLC Employment International Transaction Guide.

Jamie completed the Post Graduate Diploma in Law at the College of Law in London. Between qualifying as a Solicitor in 2007 and joining Ommar Platinum Consults in 2013, Jamie worked for a number of niche employment law firms in the UK including the UK’s largest specialist employment law firm – Doyle Clayton Solicitors.

  • ARE POST-TERMINATION RESTRICTIONS WORTH THE PAPER THEY’RE WRITTEN ON?

    In this article, Jamie Liddington addresses some of the questions which employers frequently pose when dealing with post-termination restrictions. A common fear among employers is the risk that senior employees (in particular, those in possession of confidential information or who have formed close client or customer connections) leave to join a competitor or set up their own competing business. While Federal Labour Law No.8 of 1980 (as amended) (the “Labour Law”) includes provisions which seek to assist employers in trying to prevent this risk from materialising, it remains a vexed topic among employers as the application of the Labour Law in relation to this topic often gives rise to uncertainty.
