ASHRAF SAYED

Partner

Dubai

+971 4 4292999

a.sayed@opconsults.com
  download vcard

Ashraf is Head of Commercial Property in Dubai. He advises on a range of real estate and commercial matters, including real estate developments, Islamic finance and international franchising. Ashraf represents a variety of clients including government entities, master developers, investors, HNWI and financial institutions. He advises on contentious as well as transactional matters including sale and purchase of high value real estate, joint ventures, property management arrangements, real estate finance, property litigation, corporate structuring and all aspects of project initiation and site acquisition, to post-project completion and management.

Ashraf also acts on commercial matters for international and regional franchisors, distributors, hotel operators, developers and service providers in the UAE. He specialises in all forms of strategic and operational joint ventures, franchise, agency and commercial law advice. In particular, Ashraf regularly advises master franchisors regarding their rights and obligations under relevant agreements.

Prior to joining Ommar Platinum Consults, Ashraf worked for a leading international law firm in London. He completed his LLB at Brunel University and the Legal Practice Course at the College of Law, in the UK, before qualifying as an English Solicitor. He is fluent in English, Hindi and Urdu.

  • HAS DUBAI'S ESCROW LAW SUCCEEDED IN SECURING PURCHASERS' MONEY?

    Dubai Law No.(8) of 2007 On the Matter of Escrow Accounts for Real Estate Development (the Escrow Law) was issued to regulate developers and safeguard purchasers’ money in respect of off-plan property purchases in Dubai. However, recently there have been cases where purchasers have been left without recourse to claim return of payments made where the project they invested in failed to complete. Ashraf Sayed examines the protections the Escrow Law offers to purchasers and whether the Escrow Law has succeeded in its objectives.
